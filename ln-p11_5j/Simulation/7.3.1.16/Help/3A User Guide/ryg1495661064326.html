Test sharpness and accuracy for a given scene

In these tests, you compare the sharpness of two sets of images at various distances while also comparing the accuracy of several AF parameters. One set of images uses the full-sweep search while the other set uses the single AF algorithm.

Setup: Place the ISO 12233 chart at the distances specified in the procedure. Place the device in a landscape orientation on a tripod. Use a 500+ lux light condition.

  1. Enable the full-sweep search and AF core logging (see Section 17.1).
  2. Open the camera and capture images at 10, 14, 20, 40, 50, 60, 120, and 200 cm.
  3. Search the logs and record the focus value and final lens position for each distance.
  4. Disable the full-sweep search, which thereby enables the AF algorithm.
  5. Open the camera and capture images at 10, 14, 20, 40, 50, 60, 120, and 200 cm.
  6. Search the logs and record the focus value and final lens position for each distance.
  7. Compare the sharpness by comparing the focus values recorded for the two sets of images (full sweep vs. algorithm).
  8. Check the accuracy by comparing the final lens positions for the two sets of images using the following equation:
    (lens position from step 3 – lens position from step 6) ≤ 2 * fine_step_size
