Conduct AF sanity tests

Conduct a sanity test whenever changes are introduced to either the actuator or algorithm. This test covers the basic checks for lens DAC range validity. Many unwanted trade-off or side effects of can be avoided if sanity tests are performed frequently.

  1. Enable full-sweep search.
  2. Capture test images:

    • Face-forward orientation – Focus on infinity and macro and capture images for both distances.

    • Face-down orientation – Focus down on an infinity scene and capture an image.

    • Face-up orientation – Focus up on a macro object and capture an image.

  3. Verify that all images are in focus.
  4. Do one of the following:

    • If they are all focused, disable full-sweep and continue to the next step.

    • If any of the images captured with full-sweep search are not focused, verify the settings in the AF Tuning tab and ensure that the linear DAC range is covered.

  5. Enable the appropriate AF algorithm and repeat step 2 to 3.
  6. Compare the two sets of images (full-sweep search vs. algorithm). The sharpness should be similar.
  7. If all images from full-sweep are focused but images obtained with the algorithms are not, repeat the sharpness, consistency, and speed tests.
