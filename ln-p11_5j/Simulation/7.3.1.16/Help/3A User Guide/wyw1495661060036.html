Verify FV stats

This procedure verifies that FV stats are generated and passed to the core algorithm. This decouples the issues between actuator and algorithm and is useful when debugging.

  1. Take note of what stats (software or hardware) the project uses.
  2. Enable AF logging.
  3. Start logging.
  4. Open and hold the camera. After focus completes, wave your hand in front of the camera. Repeat five times.
  5. Observe the FV values (corresponding to your stats type).
  6. Verify that they are nonzero and that they are changing monotonically.
  7. Verify that the contrast of the FV is large enough when it is in-focus and out of focus (typically in-focus FV can be approximately 2x to FV out of focus).
The algorithm often invalidates certain frames and discards stats because of the physics of the lens movement. This is normal behavior.
  • If all stats are 0 for every frame, then the test did not pass and there is a potential software issue.
  • If software stats are 0 randomly, it may indicate that the software stats computation is slow. If no other optimization for CPU usage can be added, then consider falling back to hardware stats.
