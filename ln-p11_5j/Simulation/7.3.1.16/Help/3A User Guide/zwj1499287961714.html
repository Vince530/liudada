Tune anti-banding vs FPS

Antibanding makes the exposure times multiples of 1/100s (50 Hz flicker) or 1/120s (60 Hz flicker). This may affect the minimum FPS requirement at 15/30 FPS etc. when 50 Hz, and exposure time is close to 1/15 or 1/30. This is due to minimal vertical blanking time that must be inserted to the frame time. By tuning this option, the user can choose what behaviors they want from AEC to handle the priorities of minimum FPS and antibanding. By default, antibanding takes priority, and FPS may drop a bit from the minimum FPS when needed.

On the AECtab, choose one of the following from the Antibanding vs FPS menu:
  • Legacy Allow FPS To Drop – Use if FPS is not a concern; allows full compensation of anti-banding.
  • Strictly Enforce FPS – Use if there is a strict FPS requirement; allows guaranteed FPS, but anti-banding may fail.
  • Maximize Exposure Time – Use if FPS can drop slightly; allows FPS to drop below the requirement slightly and anti-banding is not always fully compensated.
