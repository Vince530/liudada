AF failure rate is too high for night/low light/flat/low contrast scenes

If overall fail rate is too high compared to reference devices, the following options are listed from least to most in terms of tuning effort and impact/trade-off.

  • Increase FV curve by tuning FV_min
  • Tune ROI for low light

Increase FV curve by tuning FV_min

FV_min tuning has the trade-off of lowering contrast in a normal scene. Because of this, tune this parameter carefully. When FV_min is set too high, it filters out some low texture edges. When set too low, noise lowers the contrast of the curve. If FV_min was not previously tuned, it can be tuned to increase contrast.

  1. Follow the procedure to tune FV_min.
  2. Verify by comparing the focus curve minimum/maximum ratio in the log. The ratio should be higher with new settings.
  3. Verify the new setting in normal scenes to ensure that it does not increase the focus failure rate.

Tune ROI for low light

Increasing the ROI size gives more samples for the FV, thus increasing the success rate. Edge contrast in low light is typically lower due to low signal. Therefore, adjusting ROI could increase success rate. The ROI clipping ratio should not be greater than 1/3 of the CAMIF, otherwise, it starts to break the focus for all other normal scenes.

  1. Set the h_clip_low_light and v_clip_low_light ratios to 0.25.
  2. Test the focus success rate for a problematic scene.
  3. Increase the ratio by 0.05 for each iteration to improve focus success rate.
  4. Verify that the ROI is not too large by focusing on a scene with multiple objects. If the focus fails, it means that the ROI contains multiple objects. If so, then reduce the ROI unless corner case focus is preferred.
