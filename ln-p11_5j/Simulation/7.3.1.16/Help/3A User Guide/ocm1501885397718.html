Low light shot

With LLS, darker scenes can use lower gains and longer exposure times, and avoid triggering the flash by adjusting the exposure times and gains.

LLS is disabled by default. Enable image stablization to avoid blurry images from extending the exposure time. For better image quality (lower gain), use a longer exposure time for exposure in low light conditions. Also, the flash trigger can be delayed to trigger the flash at lower lighting conditions.

AEC outputs the LLS capture parameter (required exposure time for LLS and suggested capture type). During capture request, if there are no LLS capture requests, AEC outputs normal exposure settings. If LLS is requested, AEC uses the calculated exposure time for LLS and uses it along with optical image stabilization (OIS).
