GIC tuning concepts

Some Bayer sensors may have a strong imbalance between the Gb and Gr channels. The artifacts caused by this imbalance may not be fully corrected by the ABF noise reduction block in the Bayer domain. Green imbalance correction (GIC) removes the banding artifacts caused by the different sensitivities between Gr and Gb channels.

Figure : (L) Sensor showing gr/gb mismatch due to different channel sensitivity creating a gr/gb imbalance artifact. (R) Sensor without gr/gb imbalance, channels have same gr/gb sensitivity.

Figure : (R) Enlarged sky insert from left image shows vertical banding caused by an imbalance between the gr and gb channels in the Bayer domain.

Figure : (L) Global green imbalance pattern. Global grid patterns, uniform throughout the image. (R) Local green imbalace artifacts. Local cross hatch artifacts that typically show up in uniform areas; very common in noisy sensors.

The Qualcomm Spectra 2xx GIC module combines green imbalance correction with peak noise reduction (PNR). The PNR submodule is used to remove peak noise in flat regions. Imbalance between the Gb and Gr channels is corrected first. Corrected Gb and Gr channels then work with R and B channels to reduce peak noise.
